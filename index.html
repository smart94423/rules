Open Source Software Forum Rules 🛡

❌ Asking for help — for problems unrelated to our tools.

We have this error coming from tool X, can you help?
Ask tool X instead of asking us.
Our app is broken and we don't know why, can you help?
Only ask us if you have evidence that your problem is related to our tools.

❌ Asking for help — without having tried, really tried.

The golden rule here is to try, try, and try again before asking us. You should spend at least a couple of hours on your specific problem before asking us.

That said, criticism is more than welcome and our tools should be ergonomic. Let us know about paper cuts and we'll fix them.

✅ Everything else is welcome.

  • Bug Reports.
    • We have this error coming from your tool.
    Give us the full error stack and we'll fix the bug.
  • Feature Requests.
  • Critism.
    We believe this aspect of your tool is not ergonomic.
    We are happy to discuss DX improvements.
    This part of the documentation is lacking / difficult to understand.
    Thanks for the feedback — we'll improve the docs.
  • Use case exploration.
    Can we use your tools to achieve what we want?
    We are happy to discuss your use case and whether our tools support it.
  • Asking for help.
    We have this problem. We tried this, that, and that other thing, but without success. Can you help?
    We can tell you did your homework before asking 👌. We are happy to help.
