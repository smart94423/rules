Open Source Forum Rules 🛡

❌ Asking for help — for problems unrelated to our tools.

We have this error coming from tool X, can you help?
Ask tool X instead of asking us.
Our app is broken and we don't know why, can you help?
Only ask us if you have evidence that your problem is related to our tools.

❌ Asking for help — without having tried, really tried.

The golden rule here is to try, reasarch, try, research, and try & research again before asking us. You should spend at least a couple of hours on one specific problem before asking for help. If you are well versed with our tools then, and only then, can you reduce your research time before asking us.

Only expert users can ask after only 10 minutes of research. If you are not well versed with our tools the answer is the vast majority of the time: read more documentation, and try longer.

That said, criticism is more than welcome and our tools should be ergonomic. Let us know if you believe that a particular aspect of our tool is not user friendly and we'll fix it.

✅ Asking for help — you did your homework.

We have this problem. We tried this, that, and that other thing, but without success. Can you help?
We can tell you first tried to solve your problem on your own before asking us — you did your homework 👌. We are happy to help.

✅ Everything else is welcome.

  • Bug Reports.
    • We have this error coming from your tool.
    Give us the error message and the stack trace; we'll fix the bug ASAP.
  • Feature Requests.
  • Criticism.
    We believe this aspect of your tool is not ergonomic.
    We are happy to discuss DX improvements.
    This part of the documentation is lacking / difficult to understand.
    Thanks for the feedback — we'll improve the docs.
  • Use case exploration.
    Can we use your tools to achieve what we want?
    We are happy to discuss your use case and whether our tools support it.
  • ...
